Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Turmeric is a perennial flowering plant widely valued for its bright yellow rhizome and medicinal properties. Known as the “Golden Spice,” it has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and cooking. It features broad green leaves and underground stems called rhizomes. The rhizome is the most commonly used part for culinary and herbal preparations.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Zingiberaceae
Genus: Curcuma
Species: C. longa

Global Spread

Native to South Asia and widely cultivated in India, Southeast Asia, and tropical regions worldwide.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in warm, humid tropical climates with consistent rainfall and good sunlight.

Medicinal Uses

Supports immunity, reduces inflammation, aids digestion, promotes wound healing, and enhances skin health.

Traditional Uses

Used in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine for treating infections, joint pain, respiratory issues, and digestive disorders.

Health Benefits

Acts as a powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent, promoting overall wellness and vitality.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 8–11
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Partial Sun to Full Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained, fertile loamy soil
Water Needs: Moderate watering

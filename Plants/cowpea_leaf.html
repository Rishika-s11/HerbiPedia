Cowpea (Vigna unguiculata)

Cowpea is a warm-season legume widely cultivated for its edible beans and nutritious leaves. Commonly known as black-eyed pea, it is valued as both a food crop and forage plant. It features trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods containing protein-rich seeds. The seeds and tender leaves are most commonly used for culinary purposes.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Vigna
Species: V. unguiculata

Global Spread

Native to Africa and widely cultivated in Asia, the Americas, and tropical regions worldwide.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in warm, semi-arid to tropical climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Medicinal Uses

Traditionally used to support digestion, improve nutrition, and strengthen overall health.

Traditional Uses

Used as a staple food, animal feed, green manure, and soil-enriching crop in sustainable farming systems.

Health Benefits

Rich in protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals, promoting heart health and balanced nutrition.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 8–11
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Full Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil
Water Needs: Moderate watering

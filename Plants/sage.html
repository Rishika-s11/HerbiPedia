Sage (Salvia officinalis)

Sage is a perennial evergreen subshrub known for its grayish leaves and woody stems. Respected for centuries for its medicinal and culinary properties, it is often called the herb of "wisdom." It produces spikes of purple or blue flowers and has a distinct, earthy aroma. The leaves are harvested year-round for use in teas, seasonings, and therapeutic extracts.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Lamiaceae
Genus: Salvia
Species: S. officinalis

Global Spread

Native to the Mediterranean region but naturalized and cultivated globally in temperate climates.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in sunny, dry environments with well-circulated air and protection from extreme humidity.

Medicinal Uses

Enhances cognitive function, relieves sore throats, supports oral health, and manages menopausal symptoms.

Traditional Uses

Historically used to treat respiratory infections, digestive distress, and as an antiseptic for skin wounds.

Health Benefits

Rich in volatile oils and polyphenols, it acts as a potent antioxidant and anti-microbial agent.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 4–10
Climate Zones: Mediterranean, Temperate
Exposure: Full Sun
Soil Type: Sandy, well-drained soil
Water Needs: Low to moderate watering

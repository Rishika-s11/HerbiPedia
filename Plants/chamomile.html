Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Chamomile is a gentle flowering herb widely known for its calming properties and daisy-like flowers. It is commonly used in herbal teas, skincare, and natural remedies for relaxation and sleep support. The plant features delicate white petals with yellow centers and grows well in sunny environments. Chamomile has been valued in traditional medicine for centuries for its soothing and healing qualities.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Matricaria
Species: M. chamomilla

Global Spread

Native to Europe and Western Asia, now cultivated worldwide in temperate regions for herbal and medicinal use.

Preferred Climate

Prefers mild, temperate climates with full sunlight and well-drained soil conditions.

Medicinal Uses

Commonly used to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, support digestion, and soothe minor skin irritations.

Traditional Uses

Used in herbal medicine for calming nerves, easing stomach discomfort, and preparing soothing teas and oils.

Health Benefits

Known for anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and calming properties that support overall wellness and relaxation.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 4–9
Climate Zones: Temperate
Exposure: Full Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained, sandy to loamy soil
Water Needs: Light to Moderate watering

Croton (Codiaeum variegatum)

Croton is a popular ornamental plant known for its vibrant, multi-colored foliage. It features thick, glossy leaves with striking shades of green, yellow, red, and orange. Commonly grown as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant, it adds a tropical touch to gardens and homes. Crotons thrive in warm climates and are valued for their bold appearance rather than medicinal use.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Euphorbiaceae
Genus: Codiaeum
Species: C. variegatum

Global Spread

Native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands, widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Preferred Climate

Grows best in warm, humid environments with consistent temperatures and protection from cold drafts.

Ornamental Uses

Commonly used for landscaping, decorative pots, indoor décor, and tropical garden designs due to colorful foliage.

Traditional Uses

Primarily valued as a decorative plant; in some regions used in cultural landscaping and aesthetic gardening.

Plant Benefits

Enhances visual appeal of spaces, contributes to indoor greenery, and improves overall ambiance in homes and offices.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 10–12
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Bright Indirect Light to Partial Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained, fertile soil
Water Needs: Moderate watering, keep soil slightly moist

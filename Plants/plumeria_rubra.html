Plumeria Rubra (Plumeria rubra)

Plumeria rubra is a deciduous flowering tree widely admired for its fragrant and colorful blossoms. Commonly known as Frangipani, it is popular in tropical gardens and landscaping. It features thick succulent branches and large elongated leaves. The flowers are most valued for ornamental, cultural, and decorative purposes.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Apocynaceae
Genus: Plumeria
Species: P. rubra

Global Spread

Native to Central America and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in warm, sunny tropical climates with well-drained soil and minimal frost exposure.

Medicinal Uses

Traditionally used in herbal remedies for skin conditions, inflammation, and mild digestive issues.

Traditional Uses

Used in cultural ceremonies, flower garlands, perfumes, and ornamental landscaping.

Health Benefits

Contains natural compounds with anti-inflammatory and soothing properties in traditional practices.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 9–11
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Full Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil
Water Needs: Moderate watering

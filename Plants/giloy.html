Giloy (Tinospora cordifolia)

Giloy is a perennial climbing shrub widely used in Ayurveda for its powerful medicinal properties. Known as “Amrita” meaning the root of immortality, it helps strengthen immunity and detoxify the body. It has heart-shaped leaves and grows naturally in tropical regions. The stem is most commonly used for preparing herbal medicines.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Menispermaceae
Genus: Tinospora
Species: T. cordifolia

Global Spread

Native to India and widely found in tropical regions of Asia including Sri Lanka, Myanmar, and China.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in warm, tropical and subtropical climates with moderate rainfall.

Medicinal Uses

Boosts immunity, reduces fever, supports digestion, manages blood sugar levels, and improves liver function.

Traditional Uses

Used in Ayurvedic medicine for treating chronic fever, respiratory disorders, arthritis, and skin diseases.

Health Benefits

Acts as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and detoxifying agent for overall wellness.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 9–11
Climate Zones: Tropical, Subtropical
Exposure: Partial Sun to Full Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained, loamy soil
Water Needs: Moderate watering

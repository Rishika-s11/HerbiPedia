Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Licorice is a herbaceous perennial legume renowned for its sweet-tasting roots. Known as “Mulethi,” it has been a staple in traditional medicine for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects. It features pinnate leaves and small, pale whitish to purplish flowers. The extensive root system is harvested, dried, and processed for its powerful therapeutic compounds.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Glycyrrhiza
Species: G. glabra

Global Spread

Native to Western Asia and Southern Europe, and widely cultivated in India, Iran, and the Mediterranean.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in sunny areas with hot, dry summers and mild winters, requiring deep soil for root growth.

Medicinal Uses

Soothes sore throats, eases digestive discomfort, supports adrenal health, and reduces skin inflammation.

Traditional Uses

Used in Ayurveda and Greek medicine to treat coughs, bronchitis, stomach ulcers, and respiratory issues.

Health Benefits

Acts as a natural expectorant, anti-viral, and hepatoprotective agent for overall immune and liver support.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 7–10
Climate Zones: Temperate, Arid, Mediterranean
Exposure: Full Sun
Soil Type: Deep, well-drained, sandy loam
Water Needs: Moderate (deep watering)

Coriander (Coriandrum sativum)

Coriander is an aromatic annual herb widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Commonly known as “Dhania”, it is valued for both its fresh leaves and dried seeds. The plant has soft, feathery green leaves and produces small white or pink flowers. It is appreciated for its digestive, antioxidant, and nutritional properties.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Apiaceae
Genus: Coriandrum
Species: C. sativum

Global Spread

Native to Southern Europe and North Africa and cultivated widely across India, Asia, and Latin America.

Preferred Climate

Grows best in cool to moderate climates and prefers mild temperatures for optimal leaf growth.

Medicinal Uses

Supports digestion, reduces bloating, helps manage cholesterol levels, and acts as a natural detoxifier.

Traditional Uses

Used in traditional remedies for indigestion, joint pain, appetite loss, and mild infections.

Nutritional Benefits

Rich in vitamins A, C, and K, along with antioxidants that support overall health.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 3–11
Climate Zones: Temperate, Mediterranean
Exposure: Full Sun to Light Shade
Soil Type: Well-drained, fertile soil
Water Needs: Regular watering

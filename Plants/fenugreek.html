Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum)

Fenugreek is an annual herb that has long been cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes. Known as “Methi,” it is celebrated for its ability to regulate metabolic health and enhance digestion. It features small green leaflets and produces pods containing pungent, amber-colored seeds. The fresh leaves and dried seeds are both staples in traditional wellness practices.
Plant Attributes & Uses

Scientific Classification

Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Trigonella
Species: T. foenum-graecum

Global Spread

Native to the Mediterranean region and Western Asia, now widely grown in India, North Africa, and Europe.

Preferred Climate

Thrives in cool to moderate climates and is often grown as a winter crop in tropical regions.

Medicinal Uses

Regulates blood sugar levels, supports lactation in mothers, aids digestion, and reduces appetite.

Traditional Uses

Used in Ayurveda and TCM for treating digestive issues, skin inflammation, and respiratory congestion.

Health Benefits

Provides a rich source of fiber and minerals, acting as a natural metabolic booster and anti-inflammatory.

Garden Information

Hardiness: Zones 6–11
Climate Zones: Temperate, Arid, Subtropical
Exposure: Full Sun
Soil Type: Well-drained, neutral pH soil
Water Needs: Moderate watering
